The National Scholarship Programme

Students from low income families – households earning £25,000 or less - can apply to their university to see if they are eligible for support under the National Scholarship Programme.

The programme is part of the government’s policy to get more young people from disadvantaged backgrounds into higher education – in particular into the country’s most selective universities. The support they’re offering can be:

  • a fee waiver or discount
  • a free foundation year
  • discounted accommodation or other types of university help
  • a maximum of £1,000 in cash

Students must apply for an award for their first year of university. Each eligible student will receive a benefit of at least £3,000 if they are a full-time student (pro rata for part-time students).

Funding the programme

We will provide funding each financial year:

  • £50 million in 2012 to 2013
  • £100 million in 2013 to 2014
  • £150 million from 2014 to 2015

Each university must provide additional funding for the programme. This is known as ‘match funding’. The National Scholarship Programme is open only to English higher education institutions that are publicly funded.

Monitoring the programme

The programme is run by the Higher Education Funding Council for England (HEFCE). Higher education institutions will have to supply data to show which students they are funding.
