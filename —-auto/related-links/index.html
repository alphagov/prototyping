Quick answer Offering credit to consumers: the law

Not what you're looking for? ↓

You need a licence from the Office of Fair Trading (OFT) to offer credit to consumers.

How to apply

The OFT will consider if you’re a ‘fit person’ to have a licence. This includes looking at your past conduct and personal integrity. The OFT may consider that you’re not a fit person if you or any of your associates have:

  • breached the Consumer Credit Act or any other consumer protection law
  • committed a criminal offence involving fraud, dishonesty or violence
  • filed for insolvency or bankruptcy
  • been disqualified from being a director

OFT has more information about the requirements for getting a licence (on oft.gov.uk).

What you need a consumer credit licence for

You may need a licence if you:

  • sell goods or services on credit (including hire purchase)
  • hire or lease out goods for more than 3 months
  • lend money
  • issue credit cards
  • arrange credit for other people
  • collect or purchase  consumer credit debts
  • give advice about people’s credit rating
  • help people find and correct records about their financial standing
  • administer agreements for creditors or  assignees (but don’t collect debts)
  • introduce customers to someone else who will lend them  money
  • help people with debt problems

Any business offering credit or financing to customer must be licensed by the OFT, not just credit specialist companies. This includes non-profit organisations.

Business to business lending

You don’t need to be licensed if you only offer credit to another business, unless your customer is:

  • a sole trader
  • a partnership with fewer than 4 partners
  • an unincorporated association

If you offer credit to these customers, you probably need a licence.

Credit reference agencies always need a licence, even if they only deal with businesses.

About consumer credit law

Find out more about consumer credit on the i OFT website (oft.gov.uk).
